Identity

Being Queer

I use they/them pronouns, and I'm transgender/non-binary.

My identity is important to me, and it's driven me to learn about the queer community. I'm interested in queer history, symbolism, representation, language, and more.

I do a lot of student activism, and I collaborate with other LGBTQ+ students in Oregon to encourage queer solidarity and safety.



Hobbies and Activities

Houseplants

I've had hundreds of houseplants over the past few years. The hobby started in 2019, when I picked up four houseplants from Dennis 7 Dee's, a local plant store. Since then, my collection has exploded in size. I constantly research houseplants, and I can talk for hours about them. I've become an expert on houseplants and their care.

If you have a dying succulent on your desk and you want to know what's wrong with it, don't hesitate to reach out! I'd love to diagnose the problem and help you solve it.

Basketry

I started doing basketry as a camper at Camp Willowbrook, an arts summer camp. I got a job there in 2021, and taught basketry to kids aged 5-10.

Now I do basketry on my own. I love using weaving to make art, and I'm creating a portfolio of my work in art class right now. I like contemporary basketry, advanced weaving techniques, and wire weaving.

Choir

I've been singing for five years, and I've been in multiple choirs. One was Bridging Voices, a chorus for queer youth in Portland. I sang with Bridging Voices for several years, and we even performed the National Anthem at a Trailblazer's game at the Moda Center.

I also sang in my school's choir, Maverick Choir (Riverdale's mascot is a maverick). We were a small group, but we were loud. I sang a solo at our performance, it was a wonderful experience.
